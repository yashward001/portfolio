## Problem

Manual options strategy execution is difficult under high volatility because signal quality shifts quickly and traders must balance opportunity with strict downside controls.

## Constraints

- Non-stationary financial time-series behavior
- Limited tolerance for unstable policy behavior
- Need to combine price signals with external sentiment context
- Real-time ingestion requirements for actionable updates

## Approach

I built a research-to-deployment pipeline centered on long straddle setups. A DQN-based policy layer and transformer-driven sequence features were combined with sentiment inputs to evaluate potential entries and exits under risk constraints.

## Architecture

- **Data layer:** streaming market and sentiment ingestion with aligned timestamps.
- **Feature layer:** rolling-window feature extraction + sequence embeddings.
- **Policy layer:** DQN agent trained for risk-adjusted action selection.
- **Evaluation layer:** out-of-sample backtests and scenario stress checks.

## Key Technical Choices

1. **DQN for sequential decision policy:** suitable for action-value optimization under evolving states.
2. **Transformer feature encoder:** stronger temporal representation than hand-crafted indicators alone.
3. **Risk constraints in decision loop:** bounded exposures and controlled strategy behavior.

Tradeoff: model complexity increased experimentation cost, but improved flexibility across different volatility regimes.

## Results

- Automated strategy research and evaluation workflows end-to-end.
- Improved consistency of decision support under volatile conditions.
- Established reusable pipeline foundations for future options strategy variants.

## What I'd Do Next

I would implement walk-forward retraining with regime-aware policy switching and integrate broker simulation APIs for tighter production realism.
